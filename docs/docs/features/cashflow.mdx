## Overview

Cashflow Forecast provides **risk-weighted predictions** of expected recoveries based on customer risk categories. It calculates expected amounts for this week and month, along with **best case** and **worst case** scenarios.

### Recovery Probability by Risk Category

| Risk Category | Probability | Description |
|---------------|-------------|-------------|
| Low Risk | 95% | Customers with good payment history |
| Medium Risk | 75% | Customers with occasional delays |
| High Risk | 45% | Customers with significant overdue history |
| Critical Risk | 20% | Customers with severe payment issues |

### Forecast Metrics

- **Expected This Week** — Probability-weighted recovery amount for the current week
- **Expected This Month** — Probability-weighted recovery amount for the current month
- **Best Case Scenario** — All pending invoices recovered at full amounts
- **Worst Case Scenario** — Only Low and Medium risk customers pay

---

## How It Works

1. The system aggregates all pending invoices by risk category
2. Each category's pending amount is multiplied by its recovery probability
3. Weekly and monthly forecasts are calculated based on due dates
4. Best/worst case scenarios provide range estimates

### Example Calculation

```
Pending by Risk:
  Low Risk:     ₹5,00,000 × 95% = ₹4,75,000
  Medium Risk:  ₹3,00,000 × 75% = ₹2,25,000
  High Risk:    ₹2,00,000 × 45% = ₹90,000
  Critical:     ₹1,00,000 × 20% = ₹20,000

Expected Recovery: ₹8,10,000
```

## API Endpoints

| Method | Endpoint | Description |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| GET | `/api/cashflow/forecast` | Returns full cashflow forecast with all metrics |

:::tip
Use the forecast to prioritize collection efforts on High Risk customers where small improvements in payment behavior can significantly impact recovery.
:::

:::note
Forecasts are recalculated in real-time as new invoices and payments are recorded. No manual refresh is needed.
:::

:::caution
Recovery probabilities are statistical estimates. Actual recovery rates may vary based on external factors not captured in the model.
:::
