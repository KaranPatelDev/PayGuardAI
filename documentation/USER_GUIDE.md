# User Guide

## What the Product Does
PayGuard AI helps businesses manage customers, invoices, payments, follow-ups, customer risk, recovery reports, and cashflow forecasts.

## Who Should Use It
Business owners, accountants, finance staff, agencies, wholesalers, distributors, consultants, vendors, and service providers should use it when they need a structured receivables workflow.

## How to Get Started
Open the hosted app, register a business account, add customers, add invoices, monitor the dashboard, record payments, and manage follow-ups.

## Step 1: Create an Account
Use Register to create an account with your name, email, business name, business type, phone, GST number if applicable, and password.

## Step 2: Add Client/Customer Details
Go to Customers and add customer details such as business name, contact person, email, phone, address, GST number, payment terms, credit limit, and notes.

## Step 3: Add Invoice Details
Go to Invoices and add invoice number, customer, invoice date, due date, amount, tax amount, and description. You can also upload a PDF/image invoice for AI-assisted field extraction.

## Step 4: Track Pending Invoices
Use the Invoices page and dashboard to monitor pending, due soon, due today, overdue, partially paid, paid, disputed, and escalated invoices.

## Step 5: Send/Monitor Follow-ups
Use AI Follow-ups to generate follow-up content and store follow-up records. The current system records follow-ups and generated content.

**Planned / Recommended Feature:** Fully automated WhatsApp, SMS, and email sending through external provider integrations.

## Step 6: Mark Payments as Recovered
Use Payments to record payment amount, date, mode, reference number, and notes. You can also mark an invoice as paid.

## Step 7: Review Business Insights
Review dashboard KPIs, charts, customer risk, cashflow forecasts, and recovery reports.

## Best Practices
- Add customers before creating invoices.
- Keep due dates accurate.
- Record partial payments immediately.
- Record customer promises in follow-up notes.
- Review overdue invoices daily or weekly.
- Use risk categories to prioritize difficult accounts.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Creating invoices without correct customer details
- Not updating payment status after receiving money
- Ignoring promised payment dates
- Treating generated follow-up messages as sent without recording actual communication
- Delaying overdue review until cashflow becomes critical
